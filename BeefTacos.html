High Protein Beef Tacos

Flavorful ground beef with spices, topped with a greek-yogurt aioli, served in a tortilla with whatever toppings you like.

~54g Protein
30 mins
4 Servings
Skip to Recipe Card Skip to Nutrition Facts

About the Recipe

  • Use a homemade seasoning blend for the best results, it really adds to the flavor of the beef.

  • Use full-fat Greek yogurt if you want the creamiest aioli, however use reduced fat for better nutrition.

  • I highly recommend shredded cheese as a topping as it adds more protein.

About the Ingredients

  • Lean ground beef - an excellent source of protein for a satisfying and filling meal.

  • Greek yogurt - adds a creamy richness to the aioli while boosting the protein content.

  • Avocado and black beans - provide healthy fats and fiber to support digestion and overall health.

Instructions

Sauté the Onion and Garlic: In a large skillet or pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely diced onion and cook for 3-5 minutes, or until softened and translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

Brown the Beef: Add the lean ground beef to the skillet. Use a wooden spoon to break up the meat as it cooks. Once the beef is no longer pink, drain any excess fat.

Create the Taco Filling: Stir in the rinsed and drained black beans, taco seasoning, tomato paste, and canned tomato. Mix well until all ingredients are thoroughly combined and the meat is covered in seasoning.

Simmer: Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for 5-10 minutes. Stir occasionally until the liquid has mostly absorbed and the mixture is juicy but not watery.

Prepare the Aioli: While the meat simmers, prepare the aioli. Add all aioli ingredients (Greek yogurt, avocado, cilantro, garlic, lime juice, salt, and pepper) to a high-speed blender or food processor. Blend until the mixture is completely smooth and creamy.

Adjust the Aioli: If the aioli is too thick, add cold water one tablespoon at a time and blend again until you reach your desired consistency. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. For the best flavor, chill the aioli in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Assemble and Serve: Warm your taco shells or tortillas according to package instructions. Fill each with a generous portion of the taco meat mixture. Top with the prepared avocado-cilantro aioli and your favorite toppings, such as shredded cheese, lettuce, tomatoes, and salsa.
